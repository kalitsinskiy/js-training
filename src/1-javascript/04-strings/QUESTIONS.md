# Week 8: Strings - Evaluation Questions

**Evaluation Format:**
- Total questions: 10
- Students: 5 candidates
- Each student gets: 2 questions randomly
- Prepare answers for ALL 10 questions!

---

**Q1:** What's the difference between `slice()`, `substring()`, and `substr()`? Which is preferred and why?

**Q2:** How do template literals differ from regular strings? Give examples of use cases where template literals are particularly useful.

**Q3:** Strings are immutable in JavaScript. What does this mean? How do you "change" a string?

**Q4:** Explain the difference between `indexOf()` and `includes()`. When would you use each?

**Q5:** How does string comparison work in JavaScript? What is the result of `'B' > 'a'` and why?

**Q6:** Explain `split()` and `join()`. How can you use them together to manipulate strings?

**Q7:** What is the difference between `replace()` and `replaceAll()`? How do you replace all occurrences using regex?

**Q8:** Explain `padStart()` and `padEnd()`. Give practical examples where they are useful.

**Q9:** What does `trim()`, `trimStart()`, and `trimEnd()` do? When are they useful?

**Q10:** How can you convert between strings and other types? Compare `Number('42')`, `parseInt('42px')`, and `+'42'`.

---

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Clear explanation of concepts
- Ability to provide code examples
- Understanding of "why", not just "what"
- Real-world use cases
